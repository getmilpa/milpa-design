questions

Before you fill the barn

The short answers. The long ones live in the docs.

What exactly does Troje back up?

Everything your contracts declare as source of truth: module state, database, event streams and uploaded artifacts.

Because Troje reads Milpa module contracts, it also knows what can be re-harvested — builds, caches and generated files stay out of the barn.

How fast is a restore?

Median full-app restore is 38 seconds. Point-in-time is one command: troje restore --to 14:02 — Troje replays events on top of the nearest snapshot.

What is a restore drill?

Every night Troje restores your latest backup into a disposable shadow field, runs your smoke tests and posts the result. A backup only counts when it restores — the drill is the proof.

Where does my data live?

In your own barn: S3, GCS or a disk you control. Everything is AES-256 encrypted with per-module keys before it leaves the field — Troje holds the manifest, you hold the keys.

Does Troje work outside Milpa?

It runs best on Milpa, where contracts tell it exactly what matters. A generic adapter covers any PHP app — filesystem plus database — without the contract-aware pruning.

What happens to my backups if I cancel?

The grain is yours. Backups live in your storage as standard archives with an open manifest, and the CLI keeps restoring them for free — cancelling only stops new harvests.
