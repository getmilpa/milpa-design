piece 07 · growth

First Rain on the Terraces

The first rain of the season arrives sideways, pushed uphill by wind that has crossed the whole valley to get here. The terraces take it in order — the highest bench first, then the overflow walking down the stone lips one step at a time. By the third hour the plot sounds like a slow applause. Nothing in the field moves faster than it must.

I made this frame from the neighbouring bench with the camera under a plastic sheet, waiting for the moment the clouds thinned enough to separate the two greens: the hard green of young maize and the soft green of the bean cover between rows. One exposure, no reframe. The negative kept the whole argument.
